How Does The Internet Work?

The Internet works by connecting different networks of computers through a system of routers

How It Works

When you open a webpage, your computer sends a request to a remote server using protocols like HTTP or HTTPS. That server responds with the content you asked for — text, images, videos, and more. These communications travel across networks of routers and switches that form the Internet’s backbone.

Major Components

  • Servers: Store websites and data.
  • Routers: Direct traffic between networks.
  • ISPs: Internet Service Providers connect users to the global network.
  • Protocols: Rules that govern data exchange, like TCP/IP and DNS.

Why It Matters

The Internet has transformed education, communication, and business. It enables social connection, supports global collaboration, and powers everything from email to streaming to online learning.
