Learn how to protect your privacy and security on the internet

Cybersecurity Basics

Cybersecurity means protecting your computer, phone, and personal information from hackers or viruses. Always use strong passwords and keep your devices updated.

Example: Instead of using password123, try something stronger like MyC@tEatsTacos!45.

Protecting Your Privacy

Your personal data, like your name, address, or photos, can be used by others if you share it online. Think before you post, and keep your accounts private when possible.

  • Don’t share your location publicly.
  • Check who can see your posts on social media.
  • Use two-factor authentication for extra protection.
Example: When signing up for a new website, check if it’s secure by looking for https:// in the web address.

Online Scams

Scammers may try to trick you into clicking bad links or sharing your info. Always be careful when something looks suspicious or “too good to be true.”

  • Don’t click random links in emails or texts.
  • Ignore messages asking for money or personal details.
  • Use antivirus software and keep it updated.
